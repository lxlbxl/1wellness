Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: January 2025

This Data Processing Agreement (DPA) governs the processing of personal data on behalf of 1wellness customers in compliance with GDPR Article 28.

1. Purpose and Scope

This DPA applies when 1wellness processes personal data on behalf of its customers (data controllers). It forms part of the main service agreement between the parties.

2. Definitions

  • "Controller" means the customer entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data
  • "Processor" means 1wellness, which processes personal data on behalf of the controller
  • "GDPR" means the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679
  • "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • "Processing" means any operation performed on personal data

3. Processor Obligations

As a processor, 1wellness agrees to:

  • Process personal data only on documented instructions from the controller
  • Ensure that persons authorized to process personal data are committed to confidentiality
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
  • Assist the controller in responding to data subject rights requests
  • Notify the controller of personal data breaches without undue delay
  • Assist with data protection impact assessments
  • Delete or return personal data at the end of the service relationship
  • Maintain records of processing activities

4. Data Subject Rights

1wellness will assist the controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to data subject requests, including:

  • Right of access (Article 15)
  • Right to rectification (Article 16)
  • Right to erasure (Article 17)
  • Right to restriction of processing (Article 18)
  • Right to data portability (Article 20)
  • Right to object (Article 21)

5. Security Measures

1wellness implements the following security measures:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS/SSL)
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Physical security of data centers
  • Employee training on data protection
  • Incident response procedures
  • Regular backups and disaster recovery

6. Sub-processors

1wellness may engage sub-processors to assist in providing services. Current sub-processors include:

  • Hosting providers (cloud infrastructure)
  • Payment processors
  • Email service providers
  • Analytics providers (with consent)

Controllers will be notified of any new sub-processors and may object within 14 days.

7. International Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the EEA, 1wellness ensures appropriate safeguards are in place, such as:

  • EU Commission Standard Contractual Clauses
  • Adequacy decisions
  • Binding Corporate Rules
