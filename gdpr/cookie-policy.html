Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 2025

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use cookies, and how you can manage cookie preferences on the 1wellness website.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

Cookies do lots of different jobs, like:

  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Understanding how you use the website
  • Keeping you logged in during your session
  • Showing relevant content and advertisements
  • Analyzing website traffic and performance

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They enable basic functions like page navigation, secure access to protected areas, and form submission.

Examples:

  • Session cookies for maintaining login state
  • CSRF protection cookies for security
  • Shopping cart cookies for e-commerce functionality
  • Consent preference cookies

Legal basis: Legitimate interest (necessary for website operation)

Analytics Cookies (Performance)

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website by collecting anonymous information. This helps us improve our website and services.

Examples:

  • Google Analytics (_ga, _gid, _gat)
  • Page view tracking
  • Session duration tracking
  • Bounce rate analysis

Legal basis: Your consent

Functional Cookies (Preferences)

These cookies remember your choices and settings (like language preference, currency, and login information) to provide enhanced, personalized features.

Examples:

  • Language preference cookies
  • Currency selection cookies
  • Remember me / login persistence
  • Video player preferences

Legal basis: Your consent

Marketing Cookies (Advertising)

These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements and track the effectiveness of marketing campaigns. They may be shared with advertising partners.

Examples:

  • Facebook Pixel
  • Google Ads conversion tracking
  • Retargeting cookies
  • Campaign attribution cookies

Legal basis: Your consent

3. Cookie Duration

Cookie Type Duration Description
Session Cookies Until browser closes Deleted when you close your browser
Persistent Cookies 1 day - 2 years Remain on your device for a set period
Essential Cookies Session - 1 year Varies by function
Analytics Cookies 1 day - 2 years Google Analytics: 2 years for _ga
Marketing Cookies 30 days - 1 year Varies by platform

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages. These include:

  • Google Analytics: For website analytics (Google Ireland Limited)
  • Payment Processors: For secure payment processing
  • Shipping Partners: For delivery tracking
  • Email Services: For email communication tracking

We do not control these third-party cookies. Please refer to the respective privacy policies of these third parties for more information.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options to manage or delete cookies:

5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your preferences by cookie category

5.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions
  • Opera: Settings > Advanced > Privacy & security > Cookies

5.3 Mobile Devices

On mobile devices, you can manage cookies through your browser settings or device settings:

  • iOS Safari: Settings > Safari > Privacy & Security
  • Android Chrome: Chrome > Settings > Privacy > Clear browsing data

6. What Happens If You Disable Cookies?

If you choose to disable or reject certain cookies, some features of our website may not function properly:

  • Disabling essential cookies: The website may not function at all
  • Disabling functional cookies: Your preferences won't be saved
  • Disabling analytics cookies: We won't be able to learn from your usage
  • Disabling marketing cookies: You'll see less relevant advertisements
