Data Retention Policy

Last updated: January 2025

This policy outlines how long we retain different categories of personal data and the criteria used to determine retention periods.

1. Retention Principles

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to:

  • Fulfill the purposes for which it was collected
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Resolve disputes and enforce agreements
  • Maintain business records for accounting and tax purposes

2. Data Retention Schedule

Data Category Retention Period Legal Basis
Account Data Duration of account + 3 years Contract performance
Order/Transaction Data 7 years from transaction date Tax/legal compliance
Health Assessment Data 3 years from last interaction Consent
Wellness Plans 3 years from creation Contract performance
Communication Records 2 years from last contact Legitimate interest
Marketing Preferences Until consent withdrawn + 30 days Consent
Website Analytics 26 months (anonymized) Legitimate interest
Cookie Data Up to 2 years Consent
Support Tickets 3 years from resolution Legitimate interest
Payment Information Not stored (handled by processors) N/A

3. Special Category Data (Health Information)

Health information receives enhanced protection:

  • Stored separately from other personal data where possible
  • Access restricted to authorized personnel only
  • Encrypted both in transit and at rest
  • Deleted promptly upon request (subject to legal exceptions)
  • Never shared with third parties without explicit consent

4. Early Deletion Criteria

Data may be deleted before the end of retention periods when:

  • The data subject exercises their right to erasure
  • The purpose for collection has been fulfilled
  • Consent has been withdrawn (where consent was the legal basis)
  • The data is no longer necessary for business operations
  • Required by regulatory or legal action

5. Data Archiving

Some data may be archived (rather than deleted) when:

  • Required for legal defense or claims
  • Needed for historical or statistical purposes (anonymized)
  • Subject to litigation hold

6. Secure Deletion Methods

We use secure deletion methods including:

  • Cryptographic erasure for encrypted data
  • Secure overwrite procedures for digital files
  • Certified destruction for physical records
  • Verification of deletion completion
