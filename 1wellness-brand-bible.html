The Name

The "1" is intentional. It signals singularity of purpose: one body, one truth, one path to sustainable health. The numeral positions us as the first authority — the original alternative voice in wellness. It is not decorative. It is our philosophy made visible.

The Big Idea

The wellness industry is full of whispers. We speak in declarations. Most wellness brands soften their message for mass appeal — they hedge, they qualify, they defer. 1wellness does none of that. We have studied the research, assembled the evidence, and built protocols that work. And we say so plainly.

We exist because millions of people with PCOS, menopausal transitions, hormonal acne, and flagging male vitality have been handed prescriptions when they needed answers. We give them the answers.

"The body already knows how to heal. Our job is to restore the conditions that make healing inevitable."

Writing Rules

  • Always write in active voice
  • Use "protocol," not "tips"
  • Use "mechanism," not "trick"
  • Use "evidence," not "studies show"
  • Start with the problem, not the product
  • Speak to the whole person, not just the condition

09 — Logo System

The Mark.

The "1" acts as a vertical spine — a single point of truth. Rendered in Amber Vitality, bold and assertive, it precedes "wellness" in Forest Authority. The numeral is set at 120% the cap height of the wordmark. No flourishes. No icon. The name is the brand.

On Forest — Reversed
1wellness
On Midnight — Reversed

Logo Rules

Always Do

  • Maintain minimum clearspace equal to the x-height of "wellness" on all sides
  • Use the primary lockup at minimum 80px wide (digital) or 25mm (print)
  • Use only the three approved background contexts shown above
  • Ensure Amber "1" always reads first in left-to-right scan

Never Do

  • Distort or stretch the proportions of the wordmark
  • Apply drop shadows, outlines, or glow effects
  • Use on busy photography without a backing shape
  • Recreate in any typeface other than Syne Bold
  • Change the colour assignment of "1" or "wellness"
10 — Photography & Visual Direction

Real.
Dramatic.
Honest.

Subject Hierarchy

Images serve the message, not the aesthetic. Lead with ingredients and biological truth.

  • Tier 1: Botanical & ingredient in dramatic close-up
  • Tier 2: The human body — unretouched, in honest context
  • Tier 3: The face — real skin, real texture, real expression

Lighting

High contrast. Single dramatic natural or directional source. No ring-light softbox aesthetics. Rich shadows are welcome.

Color Treatment

Earthy, warm, desaturated mid-tones. Rich shadows. No bright white studio pops. Botanical green tones in balance with warm amber skin light.

Composition Rules

  • Asymmetric framing — subject touching the frame edge
  • Typography bleeding into the image
  • Generous negative space in the upper third for text
  • Avoid centre-weighted commercial composition

Forbidden

  • Stock photo smiles with supplement bottles
  • Pastel background flatlay photography
  • Generic "wellness spa" imagery
  • Photoshopped or AI-smoothed skin
  • Sanitised perfection of any kind

14 — Brand Don'ts

What We
Never Do.

Our standards are not optional. Every element of this list exists because someone, somewhere, has damaged trust in the wellness category by doing exactly these things. We will not.

Voice & Copy
  • Say "journey" as a metaphor for health progress
  • Use the word "toxins" without specific identification
  • Write "consult your healthcare provider" as an evasion
  • Use "natural = safe" logic without nuance and caveats
  • Shame conventional medicine wholesale
  • Write "may support" for compounds with Level A evidence
Visual Design
  • Use Canva default templates for branded materials
  • Use stock photos of smiling women with supplement bottles
  • Use pastel colour schemes — we are not soft
  • Use Comic Sans, Helvetica, Arial, or system fonts
  • Apply drop shadows to the logo
  • Use gradients without intentional, designed application
Product Standards
  • Launch any product without third-party testing documentation
  • Use proprietary blends — full label transparency always
  • Make claims we cannot substantiate with mechanism or study
  • Formulate for cost savings that compromise bioavailability
  • Mislead on dosing or forms of active compounds
Partnerships
  • Co-brand with pharmaceutical companies without board review
  • Partner with influencers who promote body-image harm
  • Accept partnerships requiring undisclosed affiliations
  • Endorse any product that contradicts our evidence standards
  • Collaborate with brands that use misleading wellness language
